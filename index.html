Gurucharan Marthi Krishna Kumar

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Neuroscience at McGill University, working with Dr. Amir Shmuel and Dr. Janine Mendola at the Montreal Neurological Institute.

My research focuses on the intersection of Artificial Intelligence and Neuroscience, particularly in medical image processing, computer vision, and deep learning applications in healthcare. I am passionate about leveraging AI to transform healthcare through innovative solutions that bridge neuroscience and medical imaging.

Currently, I am developing automated deep learning pipelines for optic pathway tractography using diffusion and structural MRI data. I have conducted over 200+ MRI acquisitions using the Siemens 3T Prisma scanner and work extensively with large medical imaging datasets using PyTorch, CUDA-enabled GPUs, and large language models.

Research

My research interests span multiple domains at the intersection of AI and neuroscience:

  • Medical Image Processing: Developing AI-driven solutions for neuroimaging analysis, particularly diffusion and structural MRI
  • Computer Vision & Deep Learning: Applications in healthcare, medical diagnostics, and optic pathway analysis
  • Generative AI: Leveraging large language models and foundation models for medical applications
  • Neuroimaging: Structural and diffusion MRI analysis, optic nerve tractography, visual pathway imaging
  • AI in Healthcare: Clinical decision support systems, automated medical image analysis
