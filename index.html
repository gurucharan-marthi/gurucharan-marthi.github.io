Gurucharan Marthi Krishna Kumar

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Neuroscience at McGill University, affiliated with the Montreal Neurological Institute. My work is supervised by Dr. Amir Shmuel and Dr. Janine Mendola. My research lies at the convergence of Artificial Intelligence, Medical Imaging, and Neuroscience. I specialize in developing automated deep learning pipelines for analyzing the optic nerve and visual pathways using diffusion MRI and structural imaging.

I’m especially interested in using foundation models and generative AI for building intelligent healthcare solutions. My broader goal is to create practical, interpretable, and scalable tools that improve medical diagnostics and neuroscientific understanding using cutting-edge deep learning techniques. Prior to McGill, I received my bachelor’s degree (with distinction) in Biomedical Engineering from Anna University, India, where I focused on medical image processing and deep learning applications.

Research

My research focuses on developing automated deep learning pipelines for medical imaging, with an emphasis on segmentation, anomaly detection, and multimodal AI integration for visual pathway analysis and optic nerve imaging.
